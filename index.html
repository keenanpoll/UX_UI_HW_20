Research

Our team started by preparing a user research plan and conducting 3 volunteer interviews and 3 parent interviews. After our user interviews we had a clear picture of who our User Persona was. The team then developed a storyboard to get an even better picture of our user. This led us to really define our problem and solution.

Problem Statement

Parents of young girls wanting to get their daughter(s) involved in the organization, as well as those interested in volunteering, had a handful of unanswered questions after visiting the website. This could lead to users becoming disinterested in GOTR, and looking for other organizations to support.

Testing & Iterations

Based on the user flow, we created low fidelity wireframes for the homepage, volunteer page, and the new parent's page. We did this for both mobile and desktop, to ensure that we had a responsive web design. We performed a handful of user tests on our low fidelity wireframes, and had each of the users complete 3 tasks. After our testing, we found a couple opportunities. For example, one was that users couldn't find the "learn more" button on theparents page. Another was that couple of the navigation items were dead ends. We made sure to fix that on our final prototype.

Final Prototype

We used the UI style guide that we had created, and implemented these elements into a final prototype. We also made appropriate changes based on the feedback we received from our initial usability testing. We built out a handful of pages for the site, for both mobile and desktop, ensuring that the page was a responsive web design, and would translate well between different devices.
